First-Aid Tips

what you should do and what you shouldn't do

Bleeding

Direct pressure and elevation

Don’t remove the cloth. Add more layers .

Burns

Running water for several minutes

  • For serious burns , Call 123
  • Apply thin fabric bandage
  • Don’t add any ointments
  • Don’t break the blisters formed

Blisters

  • Small , unbroken and not very painful : Cover it to prevent rubbing and leave it alone.
  • Large and painful : Use a sterilized needle and make small punctures at the edge of the blister and express the ﬂuid. Then apply antibiotic ointment and cover it to protect it from further rubbing and pressure.

“Limbs injury”

Treated as broken bones until an X-ray can be obtained

  • Don't try to straighten the limb
  • Apply a cold pack , but not directly on the skin
  • Stabilize the limb using a splint and padding to keep it immobile.

Cardiac Arrest

  • Call 123 immediately
  • CPR is the most important procedure (next slide )
  • Immediately start chest compressions regardless of your training.
  • Hand this task over to those who are trained if and when they arrive.

Nosebleed

  • Lean forward, not back.
  • Pinch (Hold )the nose just below the bridge.
  • Don't pinch the nostrils closed by pinching lower.
  • You can also apply a cold pack .

Fainting

If you feel faint :

  • Lie down or sit down to reduce the chance of fainting again, don't get up too quickly.
  • Place your head between your knees if you sit down.

If someone else faints :

  • Position the person on his or her back
  • To reduce the chance of fainting again, don't get the person up too quickly.
  • If the person doesn't regain consciousness within one minute, call 123
  • Check for breathing. If the person isn't breathing, begin CPR

Bee stings

  • Get the stinger out any way you can to prevent more venom being delivered.
  • Use a cold pack to reduce swelling at the site, but take care not to cause frostbite.
  • Use an antihistamine to reduce swelling and itching. Monitor the person who was stung.

Diabetic coma

((( Call 123 for an ambulance immediately)))

  • Don’t try to give them anything to eat or drink, as they may choke.
  • Turn them onto their side to prevent obstruction to breathing.
  • Follow any instructions given to you by the operator until the ambulance arrive.
  • Don’t try to give them an insulin injection.
  • If available, administer 1 mg of glucagon for rapid reversal of hypoglycaemia ( Very low blood sugar)
  • Note : You have to measure the blood glucose level Low blood sugar is much more dangerous than low one

Frostbite

The most important procedure is : gradual warming

  • If you can't make it to a medical facility, use immersion of the affected area in warm water (98 to 105 F) for 20 to 30 minutes to rewarm it.
  • Do not rub the affected area or use heat sources.
  • Small areas of minor frostbite may be rewarmed by skin-to-skin contact.
  • Avoid using any heat sources or hot packs.

Jellyfish stings

  • Rainse the area of the sting generously with vinegar/ baking soda for at least 30 seconds.
  • Do not use a pressure bandage.
  • Immerse the affected area in hot water, as hot as the person can tolerate until the pain goes away.
  • If hot water isn't available, use dry hot packs. If those aren't available, use dry cold packs.
  • Other methods to relieve pain are less effective such as urine ★ Urine only works if it is acidic. Depending on diet, urine is not always acidic. Therefore, urine does not always work.
